What to Wear to (Pretty Much) Every Occasion

Finally, your go-to guide for how to dress at different events, from weddings to wakes, and everything in between.

What are weddings, baptisms, networking gatherings, funerals, job interviews, picnics, holiday parties, baby and bridal showers all about? It's always difficult to decide what to wear to them. What you dress to any of these events is determined by a variety of circumstances, including the location, the formality of the invitation, the weather, and, in many cases, what everyone else is planning to wear. However, there are certain broad sartorial norms to which you might refer for help. (Remember, most dress code "laws" are more like guidelinesâ€”with a few exceptions, such as not wearing white to a wedding.)

Real Simple fashion editors and experts break down and interpret every potential event dress code, including practical recommendations, etiquette solutions, and outfit choices, to put an end to the what-do-I-wear conundrum once and for all.

Here's what you should wear...

Formal Events

Baptism, Bar Mitzvah, or Other Religious Ceremony

Wear a charming daytime dress, perhaps in a petal or floral print, or a suit with feminine underpinnings and accents. Bring a light sweater, blazer, or smart jacket to cover up sleeveless designs and avoid anything low cut, short, or clinging. Wear a cocktail dress or stylish slacks with a jacket or wrap to cover up in the temple or church if there is a reception immediately following the ceremony and no time to change.

Black-Tie Wedding or Charity Gala

Consider tuxedos, glimmering materials, and extravagant accents. While etiquette laws on dress length are relaxing, a tea-length or floor-length gown is your best choice. A column or A-line dress is most refined, but a well-tailored, sleek, and formal suit is also appropriate. It's time to dress up your look with beautiful jewelry, wonderful shoes, and a stunning hairdo.

If the invitation reads "black tie optional," you may relax a little. If you don't have a long gown, a formal knee-length cocktail dress is suitable.

Cocktail Party, Engagement Party, and Most Weddings

The party dress code for one of these rather formal gatherings will most likely be "cocktail attire" (although not as fancy as a black-tie reception). So go for a cocktail dressâ€”you can't go wrong with a little black dress or a jewel-toned or pastel piece. Dress it up with fancy items as well.

Cocktail parties nowadays may range from a fancy social affair (cue that sparkly knee-length number from the "special occasions" section) to a low-key gathering of pals gathered around a plate of snacks. However, "cocktail parties are dressy-casual, so you can't go wrong with a blouse with some particular embellishments and a skirt or fitted slacks, with heels or elegant flats," according to fashion expert Joseph Williamson. "Avoid materials that are too casual, such as chino, jersey, and denim," and avoid super-short fashions. To see if a hemline is acceptable, stand with your hands at your sides and check the hemline: it should not be higher than your fingers.

Another thing to keep in mind is that various cities have varying clothing codes. There's no need to overthink it, but it's worth remembering. "Regardless of the weather changes, cocktail clothing in Miami is just as formal and stylish as in New York, but in San Diego, it's interpreted a bit more casually since the city is easygoing," says Lauren A. Rothman, creator of Style Auteur, a fashion-consulting organization located in Washington, D.C.

Funeral or Wake

A pantsuit or knee-length dress with a cardigan or jacket on top is a safe bet for what to wear to a funeral. Jewelry should be delicate and unobtrusive (simple studs and a minimalist pendant necklace). This is not the time to be showy or fashionable; instead, keep things orderly, courteous, and modest (unless otherwise instructed).

In terms of color, black is a popular option, but it is not required. What matters most is conveying the solemnity of the occasion, which may be done with toned-down apparel in "dark neutrals, such as navy, brown, and forest green," according to etiquette expert Leah Ingram. Patterns that are dark and discreet are also appropriate.

More Casual Affairs

Holiday Party, Dinner Party, or "Festive Attire" Event

These events allow you to experiment with sparkle and color. So, in essence, practically anything is acceptable as long as it is appropriate for the venue and occasion. Have some fun with a bold necklace or earrings. Then, wear it with a bright dress, a sleek jumpsuit, or a festive top with a quirky midi skirt or palazzo pants. If you wear tactile accessories like feathers, tassels, lace, sequins, beading, or bows, you'll get extra points.

Bridal Shower or Baby Shower

Shower attire includes a flattering, feminine dress, a patterned skirt and sweater, or a colorful pair of leggings and a flowing top. Wear flats or heels (whatever you choose), or lovely booties if it's cold outside. Remember that bridal showers follow the same conventions as weddings: unless the bride or the shower hosts express otherwise, white should be saved for the bride. Black isn't out of the question, but make sure it's more elegant than melancholy.

Family Get-Together, Barbecue, or Birthday Party

Dress it down with a good pair of jeans, a colorful blouse, and fashionable shoes, sandals, or flats. Relaxed vibes are nice, but leave the yoga pants, graphic shirts, and worse-for-wear shoes at home. Instead, take a lead from the host: if they're constantly dressed to impress, you should, too.

On a Cruise

Pack for a warm-weather cruise what you would for a warm-weather beach trip during the day: shorts, breezy shirts, sundresses, skirts, bathing suits, coverups, sandals, and shoes. Pack a range of clothes you'd wear throughout the day on an autumn or winter vacation for a cold-weather cruise: jeans, leggings, slacks, sweaters, shoes, winter coat, boots, and flats (include a bathing suit or two if there's a hot tub or indoor pool). Be at ease and calm, but keep in mind that you are not alone at home.

Many cruise ships provide a variety of restaurant selections ranging in formality from very casual (no bathing suits without cover-ups, bare feet, or ripped jeans) to smart casual and formal. If there is only one main dining area, the dress code may vary during the voyage (for example, casual the first night, and formal or maybe even black-tie to celebrate the last night). Bring a couple different dinner outfits for each need.

For a casual meal, you can't go wrong with a knee-length sundress or clean white trousers and a lovely blouse combined with sandals or flats. For a smart-casual night, replace the white denim with finer pants, attractive jewelry, and a pair of wedges or clunky shoes. Tea-length or floor-length dresses, heels, and a pretty wrap work perfectly for formal nights. Again, some cruises incorporate a black-tie dinner, in which case, go ahead and dress to the nines.

Career Related Functions

Job Interview

Maintain a professional, clean, and suitable appearance. Pumps with a fitted suit (pants or skirt) or a plain black or blue dress are always appropriate for a job interview. These are the norm for most corporate jobs, but if you're looking for work in a more laid-back profession, such as a tech startup, design agency, or trendy magazine, feel free to exhibit your particular flair. However, being overly fashionable or informal might be unappealing. Avoid jangly, distracting jewelry, as well as denim (yes, even if the prospective company dress code is casual; you should always be more dressed up than the people who work there)..

Graduation

Don't overthink it when it comes to what to dress to a graduation ceremony. It's sleek and tidy, but not overly formal. If you're the graduate, don't wear anything you'll be embarrassed to wear in a slew of graduation photographs or during a celebration meal with your grandparents. A knee-length bright, white, or floral dress is an excellent choice for the joyful event. Also, to walk easily across the stage, wear comfortable heels, flats, wedges, or attractive sandals.

Follow the same recommendations as above for someone else's graduation, or as you would for a springtime baptism, bar mitzvah, or Easter celebration: tidy, enjoyable, and considerate, but not unduly extravagant. Try elegant cropped trousers with a good top, a pretty patterned skirt, or a bright sundress made of excellent fabric. Many graduations take place in late spring or early summer, and they sometimes entail sitting outside for lengthy grad ceremonies, so keep the weather in mind. To avoid being overheated in long sleeves or slacks, choose an airy A-line dress or skirt. If it's raining, you could be transferred to an air-conditioned theater or gymnasium, so bring a shawl or sweater to keep warm.

Business Dinner, Work Party, or Networking Event

Here's one word to guide you: polished. When dressed for a business function, the culture of your office should take precedence. "If you work in a conservative setting, dress conservatively for gatherings as well," Williamson advises.

Wear something more formal than what you'd wear to work. Wear a lovely jewelry or blouse with your suit, or go for a skirt and heels instead of slacks and flats. You are the expert on your company's dress code, so err on the conservative side, especially if you will be interacting with clients or work superiors. Dress comfortably for the events on the program for a casual outing, such as a workplace picnic or a charity 3K run. Simply avoid anything dubious or controversial (super-revealing tank tops, barely-there ripped denim shorts, or T-shirts with eyebrow-raising messaging or graphics).
